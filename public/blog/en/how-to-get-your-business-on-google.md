If someone searches for what you sell and you don't show up on Google, you're invisible to them. It's that straightforward. The good news is that learning how to get your business on Google isn't complicated, and it won't cost you anything.

Google offers a free tool called <a href="https://business.google.com/us/business-profile/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Google Business Profile</a> (formerly Google My Business) that lets any legitimate business appear on Google Search and Google Maps. Whether you run a restaurant, a plumbing service, or an online consultancy, this is the single most important step you can take for local visibility.

This guide walks you through the entire process, from creating your profile to getting verified and optimizing for more customers.

## Why getting your business on Google matters

Most people don't scroll past the first page of search results. If your business doesn't appear there, potential customers will find your competitors instead.

A Google Business Profile puts your business in front of people who are actively searching for what you offer. It shows up in the local map pack (the map with three business listings that appears at the top of local searches), in Google Maps, and in the knowledge panel on the right side of desktop results.

According to Google, businesses with complete profiles are seen as more reputable. Research from their own platform suggests that <a href="https://maps.google.com/intl/en_uk/business/articles/how-to-add-your-business-to-google-maps/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">almost 70% of users view businesses with complete listings</a> as more trustworthy. Your profile also lets customers call you, get directions, read reviews, and visit your website, all without leaving Google.

The best part? It's completely free. Creating a Business Profile and listing your business on Google costs nothing.

## How to get your business on Google step by step

The setup process takes about 15 to 20 minutes. Here's exactly what to do.

### Step 1: Go to Google Business Profile

Head to <a href="https://business.google.com/us/business-profile/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">business.google.com</a> and sign in with your Google account. If you don't have one, you'll need to create a free Google account first using either a Gmail address or your existing business email.

### Step 2: Add your business name and category

Click "Add your business to Google" and enter your business name. Google will search for existing listings. If your business already has one (customers or Google may have created it), you'll see the option to claim it. If not, you'll create a new profile from scratch.

Choose a business category that best describes what you do. This is important because it determines which searches your business appears in. Be specific: "Thai restaurant" is better than just "restaurant."

### Step 3: Add your location

If you have a physical location where customers visit you, enter your full address. Google will place a pin on the map for you.

If you're a service-area business (you go to customers instead of them coming to you), you can skip the address and define the areas you serve. Google won't display your home address publicly, which is ideal for home-based businesses and freelancers.

### Step 4: Fill in your contact details

Add your business phone number and website URL. If you don't have a website yet, you can skip this for now, but having one significantly improves your credibility and click-through rate.

### Step 5: Complete your profile

This is where most people stop, and it's a mistake. A half-finished profile performs poorly. Fill in everything Google asks for:

- **Business hours:** include special hours for holidays
- **Business description:** a clear summary of what you do (up to 750 characters)
- **Photos:** your storefront, interior, products, team, and logo
- **Services or menu items:** list what you offer with descriptions
- **Attributes:** things like "wheelchair accessible," "free Wi-Fi," or "women-owned"

The more complete your profile, the better it ranks in local searches and the more confidence it gives potential customers.

## How to verify your Google Business Profile

Google won't show your profile to the public until you verify that you actually own or manage the business. This is their way of preventing fake listings.

### Verification methods

Not every method is available for every business. Google decides which options to offer based on your business type and location.

| Method | How it works | Timeline |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Postcard | Google mails a postcard with a 5-digit code to your business address | Up to 14 days |
| Phone | You receive an automated call or SMS with a code | Instant |
| Email | Google sends a verification code to your business email | Same day |
| Video | You record a short video showing your business location and signage | A few days |

Postcard verification is the most common method, especially for new businesses. When the postcard arrives, sign in to your Business Profile and enter the code. Don't request a new code while waiting, as it invalidates the previous one.

If you're eligible for phone or email verification, those are obviously faster. Google has been rolling out video verification more widely, where you record a short clip showing your business name, location, and the surrounding area.

## Optimizing your profile for more visibility

Getting listed is step one. Making your profile work hard for you is step two.

### Add high-quality photos regularly

Businesses with photos receive more requests for directions and more clicks to their websites than those without. Upload at least five to ten quality photos when you first set up, then add new ones regularly. Show your actual space, your team, and your products or services in action.

### Collect and respond to reviews

Reviews are one of the strongest ranking factors for local search. Ask satisfied customers to leave a Google review, and always respond to reviews (both positive and negative). A thoughtful response to a negative review often matters more than the review itself.

### Keep your information accurate

Nothing frustrates a potential customer more than showing up during what Google says are your business hours, only to find the door locked. Update your hours when they change, especially during holidays and special events. Keep your phone number, address, and website current.

### Use Google Posts

Google Posts let you share updates, offers, events, and news directly on your Business Profile. They appear in your listing when people search for your business. Think of them as mini social media updates that live right in Google Search results.

### Track your performance with Insights

Google Business Profile includes built-in analytics called Insights. You can see how customers find your business, what search terms they used, and what actions they took (called you, visited your website, requested directions). Use this data to understand what's working and what to improve.

## Common mistakes that keep businesses off Google

Even after setting up a profile, some businesses struggle to appear in search results. Here are the most frequent reasons.

### Incomplete profiles

If you only filled in your name and address, Google has very little information to work with. Profiles with descriptions, categories, photos, and hours consistently outrank bare-bones listings.

### Wrong or inconsistent business information

Your business name, address, and phone number (often called NAP) should be identical everywhere it appears online, including your website, social media, and directories. Inconsistencies confuse Google and can hurt your ranking.

### Ignoring reviews

A business with zero reviews looks unestablished. Worse, a business with unanswered negative reviews looks like it doesn't care. Both situations push customers toward competitors who engage with their reviews.

### Duplicate listings

Sometimes a business ends up with multiple Google profiles (one they created, one Google auto-generated, one a former employee made). Duplicate listings split your reviews and confuse customers. Search for your business on Google Maps and claim or remove any duplicates.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Is it free to get your business on Google?**
A: Yes. Creating a Google Business Profile and listing your business on Google Search and Maps is completely free. You don't need to pay for ads or a subscription to appear in local results. Google offers this as a standard tool for any legitimate business.

**Q: How long does it take to get your business on Google after signing up?**
A: Your profile can go live within a few days if you verify by phone or email. Postcard verification takes up to two weeks since Google mails a physical code to your business address. Once verified, your listing typically appears in search results within 24 to 48 hours.

**Q: Can I get my business on Google without a physical storefront?**
A: Yes. Google Business Profile supports service-area businesses that operate at customer locations instead of a fixed address. During setup, you can choose to hide your address and define the areas you serve instead. This works well for contractors, cleaners, consultants, and similar businesses.

**Q: What information do I need to get my business on Google?**
A: You'll need your business name, address or service area, phone number, business category, and website URL. Adding your hours of operation, photos, and a business description will strengthen your profile. The more complete your listing, the more likely customers are to trust and contact you.

**Q: Does getting your business on Google help with SEO?**
A: Absolutely. A verified Google Business Profile directly improves your local SEO by making your business eligible to appear in the local map pack and knowledge panel. It also sends trust signals to Google's algorithm, which can boost your overall search visibility for location-based queries.
